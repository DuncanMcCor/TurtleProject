The Kemp's Ridley Sea Turtle (*Lepidochelys kempii*)

Endangered Species Presentation

Slide 1: Species Overview and Conservation Status

Fact Detail
**Scientific Name***Lepidochelys kempii*
**Size**Smallest sea turtle species. Adults are about $60-70$ centimeters ($24-28$ inches) in carapace length.
**Weight**Adults typically weigh $32-45$ kilograms ($70-100$ lbs).
**Appearance**Carapace (top shell) is olive-gray or grayish-green and nearly circular (as wide as it is long). Plastron (bottom shell) is pale yellow. Hatchlings are entirely dark.
**Conservation Status****Critically Endangered** (IUCN) / **Endangered** (U.S. Endangered Species Act). Once neared extinction; efforts have led to a fragile recovery.
**Diet**Primarily bottom-feeders that use powerful jaws to crush prey, mostly blue crabs, but also fish, mollusks, and sea urchins.

Slide 2: Habitat and Alabama Range

The Kemp's Ridley is strongly associated with the Gulf of Mexico, making it particularly important to the Gulf Coast ecosystem.

  • Primary Habitat: Shallow, nearshore, coastal waters and estuaries (bays and river mouths), typically less than $50$ meters ($160$ feet) deep, with muddy or sandy bottoms. These areas provide rich feeding grounds.
  • Alabama Connection: While the majority of the population lives in the western Gulf, juvenile Kemp's Ridleys are known to use **Alabama's coastal and bay waters** as seasonal developmental and foraging habitats, especially when blue crabs are abundant.
  • Nesting in Alabama: Although $95\%$ of global nesting occurs in Tamaulipas, Mexico, occasional, rare nesting events have been documented on Alabama beaches (from Dauphin Island to Alabama Point).

Slide 3: Unique Nesting Habits (The Arribada)

Kemp's Ridleys exhibit one of the most unique and vulnerable behaviors of any sea turtle species:

  • Nesting Schedule: They nest every 1 to 3 years, laying 2-3 clutches per season, with around 100 eggs per clutch. Incubation takes $\sim 45-55$ days.
  • Daytime Nesters: They are the only sea turtles that primarily nest during **daylight hours** (April to August), a trait thought to help them avoid nocturnal predators.
  • The *Arribada*: This Spanish word, meaning "arrival," describes the synchronized, mass nesting event where hundreds or thousands of female turtles come ashore on the same stretch of beach at the same time. This behavior is believed to overwhelm predators.

Did you know?

The historical *Arribada* event documented in 1947 showed an estimated 40,000 females nesting in a single day!

Slide 4: Locomotion and Tracking

Locomotion

Like all sea turtles, they use their large, powerful front flippers for efficient propulsion through the water. On land, they move via a labored, alternating flipper crawl.
